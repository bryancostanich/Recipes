# Blueberry Cornbread Muffins

These muffins have really good texture. The high egg content helps them bind and not be too dry and crumbly. 
The sour cream helps the baking soda activate and will make a nice brown color on the outside of the muffin.

Makes about (12) regular size muffins.

## Ingredients

* (1-1/2 Cup) Flour
* (1-1/2 Cup) Cornmeal
* (1 Cup) Sugar
* (2 Tsp) Salt
* (3-1/2 Tsp) Baking Powder
* (1/2 Tsp) Baking Soda
* (1 Tbsp) Cinnamon
* (3) Eggs @ Room Temperature
* (1 Cup) Milk @ Room Temperature
* (2 Tbsp) Sour Cream @ Room Temperature
* Zest of (1) Lemon
* (1/2 Cup) Melted Butter
* (2 Cups) Frozen Blueberries
* Cupcake Liners (Optional)

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 450° (Conventional) or 425° (Convection).
2. Combine flour, cornmeal, sugar, sal, baking powder, baking soda, and cinnamon in a mixing bowl and whisk until well mixed.
3. In a separate bowl, add eggs, milk, sour cream, and lemon zest and lightly mix.
4. Add bowl of liquid ingredients to dry ingredients and mix with a whisk until smooth.
5. Add melted butter and mix until the smooth.
6. Fold in the frozen berries.
7. Either put cupcake liners in cupcake baking sheet, or grease baking cups with melted butter.
8. Using a spoon, spoon the muffin mix into the cupcake cups until they're basically full.
9. Bake for 7 minutes @ 450° (Conventional Oven) or 425° (Convection Oven).
10. Reduce heat to 400° (Conventional Oven) for 15-20 minutes or 375° (Convection Oven) for ~12 minutes. 
Muffins are done when you can stick a toothpick into the center of the muffin and it comes out clean.

## Variations

* These are very cinnamon heavy. If you don't like a much cinnamon reduce to 1 or 2 Tsp.
* For more savory breakfast muffins, reduce sugar to 3/4 Cup. For dessert style, increase sugar to 1-1/4 Cup.
